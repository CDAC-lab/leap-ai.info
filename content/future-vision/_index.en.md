## Future Vision

LEAP is a platform in active evolution. As La Trobe advances toward its Net Zero 2029 goal, LEAP's roadmap extends its intelligence into new domains — deepening its research capabilities, expanding its community reach, and opening the platform to organisations beyond the university.

---

### Electric Vehicle & Battery Research

La Trobe is home to Australia's first dedicated EV research hub, supported by a $5.2M Victorian Government investment. LEAP is positioned as the intelligence layer for this facility — integrating EV charging load data, battery storage profiles and grid interactions into the platform's forecasting and optimisation models. This work will advance the practical science of renewable energy storage and demand management at scale, with implications well beyond campus.

---

### Extended Digital Twin Models

The next generation of LEAP's digital twin capability will model increasingly complex energy systems — moving from individual buildings and clusters to full campus microgrids and beyond. These richer models will enable more sophisticated simulation of energy flows, infrastructure investment scenarios and decarbonisation pathways, giving decision-makers the analytical depth to plan confidently over a 10–20 year horizon.

---

### Public Engagement Models

LEAP's open data initiative — which has already released the [UNICON](https://github.com/CDAC-lab/UNICON) and [UNISOLAR](https://github.com/CDAC-lab/UNISOLAR) datasets publicly and hosted a Net Zero Emissions Hackathon — will expand into broader community engagement models. The goal is to make La Trobe's energy intelligence accessible and meaningful to schools, community centres and the wider public, fostering energy literacy and local sustainability action.

LEAP has already developed a 3D interactive campus visualisation that renders live building-level emissions data in a spatial, publicly accessible format — showing total generation, consumption and emissions saved across the entire estate in a way anyone can explore.

---

### Innovative Human–Computer Interfaces for Energy

The next phase of COOEE's development will push further into novel interaction paradigms — exploring how people engage with complex energy data through conversation, personalisation and ambient intelligence. The aim is to make sustainability data not just accessible, but genuinely intuitive for every type of user, regardless of technical background.

---

### Availability Beyond La Trobe

LEAP was purpose-built for La Trobe's complex, multi-campus estate — but the platform's architecture, AI models and data frameworks are designed to generalise. Future plans include making LEAP available to external organisations, including other universities, local governments and facilities operators, as a replicable model for AI-driven sustainable energy management.

---

*LEAP's future is a continuation of its founding purpose: to demonstrate that artificial intelligence, applied thoughtfully and ethically to real-world infrastructure, can accelerate the transition to a sustainable, net zero future.*
