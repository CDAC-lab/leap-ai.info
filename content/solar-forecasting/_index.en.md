## Solar Forecasting

LEAP's Solar Forecasting capability delivers high-fidelity generation predictions and real-time performance monitoring across La Trobe's solar PV fleet.

### How It Works

Solar Forecasting uses two internationally recognised irradiance baselines — **NASA satellite-derived data** and **Typical Meteorological Year (TMY)** profiles — and plots them against actual hourly generation data collected from campus meters. This gives facilities managers and researchers a clear, quantified view of how the solar fleet is performing at any point in time.

### Key Capabilities

**NASA & TMY Irradiance Baselines** — Actual generation is continuously benchmarked against NASA satellite irradiance data and TMY climate profiles, providing statistically robust expected-output references.

**Hourly Generation Tracking (kWh)** — Meter-level generation is captured at hourly resolution, enabling granular analysis of output patterns throughout the day and across seasons.

**Solar Irradiance Monitoring (GHI)** — Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) is monitored in real time, providing the environmental context needed to distinguish weather-driven variation from equipment underperformance.

**Actual vs. Expected Performance** — Automated comparison of measured output against modelled expectations highlights shortfalls early, enabling proactive maintenance and reducing energy loss.

### Solar Sites Fleet Management

LEAP also provides a fleet-level health view across every solar site, showing actual generation, expected generation, days offline, avoided emissions (tCO₂e) and 7-day forecast per site. Sites flagged in red are underperforming or offline, enabling rapid prioritisation of maintenance and inspection.

![Solar Sites fleet management view showing red and green health cards per solar site with actual vs expected generation, days offline, avoided emissions and 7-day forecast](/images/leap/solar-forecasting/solar-sites-fleet.png)

### Relationship to LEAP's AI Engine

Solar Forecasting feeds directly into LEAP's Predictive AI models, which generate short-to-long term generation forecasts used for microgrid optimisation, demand scheduling, and energy market decision-making. The forecasting layer is also integrated with the COOEE AI Companion, allowing users to query solar performance in natural language.
