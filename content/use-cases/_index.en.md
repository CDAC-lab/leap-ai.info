## LEAP in Action

The following use cases demonstrate LEAP operating on live data across La Trobe's five campuses. Each represents a real operational scenario where AI-driven insights have directly informed decision-making toward net zero emissions.

---

### Campus-Wide Energy Overview

LEAP provides a unified view of energy consumption, generation, saved CO₂ emissions, and cost savings across every campus — simultaneously. Facilities managers can drill from the all-campuses summary into any individual campus or building in a single click.

![All Campuses summary dashboard showing total energy consumption, generation, CO₂ saved and cost saved across Bundoora, Wodonga, Bendigo, Mildura and Shepparton campuses](/images/leap/use-cases/all-campuses-overview.png)

At a glance: **16.4M kWh** consumed, **1.36M kWh** generated, **1.45M tonnes CO₂-e** saved, and **$158,853 AUD** in cost savings — tracked continuously across the entire estate.

---

### Actionable Insights

LEAP moves beyond monitoring into action. The actionable insights dashboard surfaces the top energy consuming days and buildings, correlated with temperature and cost, so operators know exactly where to focus.

![Actionable insights dashboard showing year-to-date energy usage down 22%, top consuming days and top consuming buildings with cost breakdown](/images/leap/use-cases/actionable-insights-dashboard.png)

The platform's AI engine doesn't just flag what happened — it traces the cause, generates a structured fault finding, and tracks the issue through to resolution and verified savings.

![Annotated diagram showing the full AI workflow: system identifies an issue, generates a fault finding, and tracks resolution through to valued outcome](/images/leap/use-cases/actionable-insight-annotated.png)

### Building Profiles & Measurement and Verification

LEAP learns the energy signature of every building. The 15-minute intra-day consumption profile below shows actual consumption plotted against three independently learned baselines — Academics, Cluster and Building — alongside ambient temperature. Deviations from expected patterns are immediately visible.

![Relative electricity consumption over the day at 15-minute resolution, showing actual consumption against Academics, Cluster and Building baselines, correlated with temperature](/images/leap/use-cases/building-profiles-intraday.png)

The long-term Measurement and Verification view extends this across years, with infrastructure events annotated directly on the chart. The impact of the LED lighting upgrade (October 2019) and two new chillers (January–February 2020) are clearly visible as step-changes in the consumption baseline.

![Long-term electricity consumption chart from 2018 to 2020 with LED Lighting upgrade and New Chiller installations annotated, showing measurable step-down in consumption after each intervention](/images/leap/use-cases/measurement-verification-longterm.png)

This is evidence-based sustainability management — every intervention quantified, every saving verified.

### Anomaly Detection

LEAP's anomaly detection engine compares hourly consumption against expected profiles and surfaces deviations as a colour-coded heatmap — green for under-consumption, red for over-consumption. Patterns that would be invisible in aggregate charts become immediately apparent at the hourly level.

![Heatmap of difference showing hourly consumption anomalies across a week, with red indicating over-consumption and green indicating under-consumption relative to baseline](/images/leap/use-cases/anomaly-heatmap.png)

Anomalies are also explained. LEAP uses SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values to identify which factors drove each anomaly — temperature, time of day, day of week, holiday status — making the AI's reasoning transparent and actionable for non-technical users.

The average daily consumption chart below, shown at hourly resolution with baseline overlay, reveals how a building's equipment draws power through the day — enabling identification of equipment running outside hours, inefficient warm-up cycles, and after-hours waste.

### Celebrating Wins

LEAP doesn't just track problems — it marks achievements. One of the platform's most significant milestones is the certification of La Trobe's Mildura campus as **carbon neutral since 2021**, powered by LEAP's continuous monitoring and optimisation.

![Mildura Campus Carbon Neutral since 2021 — powered by the La Trobe Energy Analytics Platform, designed and built by the Centre for Data Analytics and Cognition](/images/leap/use-cases/mildura-carbon-neutral.png)

This is the tangible outcome of the platform's work: not a dashboard metric, but a verified real-world achievement that demonstrates what AI-driven energy management can deliver at scale.

### Public Engagement: 3D Campus Visualisation

LEAP's public engagement layer brings energy data to life in a 3D interactive campus map. Buildings are rendered with live emissions data, allowing the community — students, staff, visitors — to understand La Trobe's sustainability performance in an intuitive, spatial context.

![3D interactive campus map showing building-level CO₂ emissions data, with a popup for the Childrens Centre showing 96.7 tonnes CO₂ emitted and a 27.58% decrease since 2018](/images/leap/use-cases/public-engagement-3d.png)

The visualisation shows total generation of 2M kWh, total consumption of 20.5M kWh, and total emissions saved of 2.4M tCO₂-e across the estate — communicated in a format accessible to anyone, not just energy professionals.
